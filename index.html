Mission

Growing STEMinists aims to pique the interest of young girls and inspire them to continue with STEM in their future studies and careers in order to close the gender gap in these fields.

Our Story

Our Founders

Ting Ting Li

Ting Ting Li is a junior in high school. She has held a passion for computer science, technology, and design since the first class she took in middle school, software applications and design. Since then she has taken many more classes as well as camps including Kode with Klossy and Girls Who Code. Additionally she is currently the co-president of her high school's Girls Who Code club. Outside of STEM, she is also a passionate advocate for many causes. She founded her own organization, Asian Advocates, and is a part of many other organizations. She is excited to tie both her passions together of advocacy and STEM to empower more girls to get involved with the STEM field.

Fiona Letsinger

Fiona Letsinger is a senior at Bethesda-Chevy Chase High School. She is in her fourth year of the engineering pathway, Project Lead the Way, a nationwide high school program offering five engineering courses through the Rochester Institute of Technology. She is also the co-president of the Rocketry Club. Fiona has fallen in love with the design process of engineering and is excited to expose girls to engaging STEM projects to share her passion for the subject. She is a competitive volleyball player and you might also find her in Norwood Park, playing with her dog Henry.
